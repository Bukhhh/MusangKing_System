Step-by-step breakdown of the segmentation algorithm

1

Gamma Correction

Why? Normalization.

Applies V_out = V_in ^ 0.6. Enhances contrast to handle varying lighting conditions across the dataset.

2

LAB Color Space

Why? Color Separation.

Converted BGR to CIELAB. Separate Lightness (L) from Color (A,B) channels for robust clustering under shadows.

3

K-Means Clustering

Why? Segmentation.

Unsupervised clustering with K=3 (Background, Husk, Flesh). Groups pixels by color similarity.

4

Morphology (Cleanup)

Why? Noise Removal.

Closing operation (5x5 kernel). Removes small holes and smooths boundaries for a clean mask.

5

Contour Detection

Why? Localization.

Finds all object boundaries. Selects the largest contour (green) as the primary durian object.

6

Feature Extraction

Calculates Area, Perimeter, and Compactness (4œÄ√óArea/Perimeter¬≤) to quantify shape.

7

Color Analysis

Computes Mean RGB and HSV values within the masked region to characterize husk color.

8

Binary Masking

Why? Final Output.

The resulting binary mask (0 or 255) used to isolate the durian pixels for the final classification model.
